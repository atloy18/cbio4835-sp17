This is a placeholder website until it can be finalized. Please excuse our dust, as it were!

This course is aimed at students:

  • in the "hard sciences" (biology mainly, though other majors are welcome!)
  • with little or no [Python] programming experience
  • who want to learn the basics of programming to help their work in biology

Topics Covered

This is an amalgam of topics that will be covered in varying levels of depth, but which will all be touched on in a broad sense. The point is to show how programming can be used to augment or even accelerate biological analyses in four distinct areas: structural biology, genomics, disease modeling, and bioimaging.

Programming: This will involve concepts like variables, loops, conditionals, functions, object-oriented programming, packages, data structures, and algorithms. It will be a little bit of a whirlwind, so be prepared!

Biology: This will involve sequence alignment and prediction, dynamic programming, structure alignment, molecular dynamics simulations, agent-based modeling, SIR disease models, image segmentation, and high-throughput imaging.

It is expected that you will be at least somewhat knowledgeable in and familiar with basic biology and biochemistry concepts (for instance, you should have a general idea of what the four topic areas bolded in the previous paragraph entail).
